Silk growing process

Seeding

Seeding is the initial stage of silk production. It involves the careful placement of silkworm eggs on a bed of mulberry leaves.

Hatching

After a few days, the eggs hatch into larvae known as silkworms. These tiny silkworms feed on mulberry leaves and grow rapidly.

Spinning Cocoons

As the silkworms grow, they start producing silk threads to create cocoons around themselves. They spin the silk in a figure-eight pattern, forming a protective casing.

Harvesting

After about two weeks, the cocoons are ready for harvesting. The cocoons are carefully collected and sorted based on quality.

Processing

The harvested cocoons are then subjected to a process called sericulture. The cocoons are boiled to kill the silkworms inside and loosen the silk fibers.

Reeling Silk

The silk fibers are carefully unwound from the cocoons using a process called reeling. The fibers are then spun together to form silk threads.

Weaving

The silk threads are woven together to create beautiful silk fabric, which can be used for various purposes like clothing, textiles, and more.
