Soybean

Soybean is a versatile crop grown for its edible seeds and oil content. It is widely cultivated around the world.

Here is some information about the lifecycle of soybean:

  • Seeding: Soybean seeds are sown in well-prepared soil during the appropriate planting season.

  • Germination: The seeds absorb moisture and sprout, sending out roots and shoots.

  • Growth: The plant grows leaves, stems, and branches. It requires adequate sunlight, water, and nutrients.

  • Flowering: As the plant matures, it produces flowers. Soybean flowers are typically white or purple.

  • Pollination: Pollination occurs when bees or other pollinators transfer pollen from the male to the female flower parts.

  • Pod Development: After successful pollination, pods containing soybean seeds begin to form.

  • Maturation: The soybean pods mature and change color. The plant's leaves may start to turn yellow.

  • Harvesting: Once the soybean pods are fully mature, they are harvested using appropriate machinery or by hand.
