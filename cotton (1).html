Growing Cotton: From Seeding to Harvesting

1. Seeding

Seeding is the first step in growing cotton. Cotton seeds are planted in the soil, usually in rows, to provide enough space for the plants to grow.

2. Germination

After seeding, the cotton seeds begin to germinate. With the right conditions of moisture and warmth, the seeds sprout, and the plants start to emerge from the soil.

3. Vegetative Growth

During the vegetative growth stage, the cotton plants develop leaves and branches. They continue to grow taller and wider, establishing a strong root system.

4. Flowering

As the cotton plants mature, they start to produce flowers. These flowers are typically white or yellow and eventually develop into cotton bolls.

5. Boll Development

The cotton bolls contain the cotton fibers. Over time, the bolls grow larger and become filled with the fluffy white fibers that are harvested to produce cotton.

6. Harvesting

Once the cotton bolls have fully developed, it is time for harvesting. The cotton plants are picked or mechanically harvested to gather the cotton fibers.
