Tea growing process

Seeding

Tea seeds are sown in well-prepared soil. They require a warm and humid environment to germinate.

Seedlings

After a few weeks, the tea seeds sprout and develop into seedlings. They are carefully nurtured in a shaded area to promote healthy growth.

Plantation

The seedlings are transplanted to the main tea plantation. They are spaced out to allow enough room for growth and proper air circulation.

Growth

The tea plants grow and develop new leaves. Regular pruning and maintenance are performed to ensure healthy growth and optimal leaf production.

Harvesting

When the tea leaves reach maturity, they are carefully handpicked or machine-harvested. The timing of harvesting is crucial for the desired flavor and quality of the tea.

Processing

After harvesting, the tea leaves undergo various processing methods such as withering, rolling, oxidation, and drying. These steps determine the type and flavor of the tea.

Packaging

The processed tea is packaged and prepared for distribution. It may be sold as loose leaves or in tea bags, depending on the market demand.
