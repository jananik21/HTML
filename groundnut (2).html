Groundnut Growing Process

1. Seeding

Groundnut seeds are planted in well-prepared soil at a depth of about 2-3 inches. Adequate spacing is maintained between the seeds to allow room for growth.

2. Germination

After planting, the groundnut seeds start to germinate. They absorb water from the soil and sprout, forming seedlings.

3. Vegetative Growth

As the seedlings continue to grow, they develop leaves and stems. The plants require regular watering and adequate sunlight for healthy growth.

4. Flowering
